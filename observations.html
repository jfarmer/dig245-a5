    What I found out about Reddit

  • I figured out that Reddit isn't exactly user friendly at the start. There isn't a tutorial or specific arrows to direct users.
  • It isn't immediately clear that a thread is or where to find it. She ran into a lot of issues opening external links instead of threads.
  • It's rather difficult to distinguish between a subreddit and a thread since both look like a bunch of comments on a page.
  • Users likely have a lot of assumptions coming from other platforms, for example, my sister's double click came from the idea of double-taping to like a picture on Instagram
  • While she managed to figure out Reddit in under 10 minutes, it still took a lot of confirmation from me to see if her actions were correct.
  • Falling into the Reddit rabbit hole is way too easy. She fell into it within her first time using it!
