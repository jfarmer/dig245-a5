  • Keep in mind my sister is 12 and hasn't used Reddit before!
  • She figured out how to create an account almost immediately, helped by the giant SIGN UP button at the top.
  • It took her another minutes to find the "night mode" switch. She clicked around until she accidentally opened settings.
  • To try and find a COVID-19 thread, she searched "coronavirus" into the search bar. She got this as the top result:
  • She clicked on the image first since it was the biggest thing there, but that just opened up the article.
  • She went back to the search result but couldn't figure out how to open the actual thread!
  • After 3 minutes, she went back to the home page by clicking the back arrow button.
  • In the homepage, she decided to scroll through all the threads, looking for a COVID-19 related one.
  • At the beginning of minute 4, she finds an r/aww post...
  • She then figured out that clicking r/aww will lead her to the subreddit and spends the rest of minute 4 looking at cute animals.

  • While in that thread, she double clicks on the first comment hoping to like it. When that gives no response, she clicks the up arrow on the right.
  • After 6 minutes, she gets back to the homepage with back arrows again but I tell her to do it with buttons instead.
